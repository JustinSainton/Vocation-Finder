# Questions for Theological/Psychological Advisor

Questions to ask a theological and/or psychological advisor to help craft this application with appropriate expertise and care.

---

## On the Psychological Framework

1. **Validity of the question categories** — Our 7 categories (service orientation, problem-solving draw, energy/engagement, values under pressure, suffering/limitation, legacy/impact, context/direction) — are these psychologically sufficient to surface genuine vocational fit, or are there blind spots? What would you add or remove?

2. **Age-appropriateness** — The target is 17–21 year olds. Are these questions cognitively and emotionally accessible to a 17-year-old high school junior, or do some assume developmental maturity that isn't there yet?

3. **Self-reporting bias** — How do we handle the gap between who someone *thinks* they are and who they actually are? Teenagers especially tend to answer aspirationally. How should the AI weigh or discount self-perception?

4. **Trauma and adversity** — Questions about suffering and limitation could surface real pain (grief, abuse, disability, poverty). What guardrails or pastoral handoffs should be built in? At what point should the app refer someone to a human counselor?

5. **Single-session reliability** — Is a 20-question written + conversational assessment enough to draw meaningful conclusions, or does vocational discernment require longitudinal data? What's the honest ceiling of confidence the AI should express?

---

## On the Theological Framework

6. **Reformed vs. broader Christian audience** — The framework is clearly Reformed (cultural mandate, providence, Luther's stations, neighbor-service). Should the app be explicitly confessional, or generalized to a broader evangelical/Catholic audience? How does that change the framing?

7. **Vocation vs. career** — How explicitly should the app distinguish between vocation (calling before God) and career (economic path)? Is there a risk that conflating the two creates spiritual anxiety for students who end up in "unspiritual-feeling" jobs?

8. **Providence framing in closed doors** — Our Q14–15 lean on Reformed providentialism. Is there psychological risk in framing obstacles as divine guidance too early in a young person's discernment? Could it discourage healthy perseverance?

9. **Pastoral role of the app** — This tool gives personalized guidance to teenagers navigating major life decisions. What's the appropriate epistemic humility the AI should model? How do we avoid the app becoming a substitute for community, mentorship, and prayer?

10. **The "17 categories" taxonomy** — Is a function-based taxonomy (healing, teaching, creating, etc.) theologically sound, or does it risk reducing vocation to function rather than person? What frameworks from vocational theology (Wingren, Hardy, Veith) should inform the categories?

---

## On Assessment Design

11. **Open-ended vs. structured** — We've chosen open-ended narrative responses over Likert scales or forced-choice items. What are the tradeoffs from a psychometric standpoint? Are there specific question types we're missing that validated instruments (like the Strong Interest Inventory or Holland codes) use that we should incorporate?

12. **The "flow state" questions** — Q7 and Q8 use flow-state framing to surface gifting. Is this a reliable proxy for gifts, or does it also surface privilege (kids with more enriched experiences have more flow memories)?

13. **Conversational audio format** — The app uses voice conversation as the primary modality. Does verbal vs. written response change what gets surfaced? Are there psychological reasons to prefer one for this population?

14. **Multi-dimensional output** — We want to avoid "you're a Type X" labels and instead give nuanced, synthesized guidance (e.g., "you're an architect called to lead"). What's the right language and tone for that output to feel empowering rather than prescriptive?

---

## On Ethics & Responsibility

15. **Data sensitivity** — Users are sharing deeply personal stories about failure, family conflict, suffering, and values. What ethical obligations does that create around data retention, privacy, and use?

16. **Who shouldn't use this app?** — Are there psychological profiles or life situations where this kind of assessment could do harm rather than good? What should the intake/onboarding screen for?
